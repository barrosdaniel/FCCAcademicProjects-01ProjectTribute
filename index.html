Ronaldo Nazario

The best football player of a generation

Playing Career Summary

  • In 1993, Ronaldo signs his first professional contract with SC Cruzeiro (BRA), aged only 16. He plays for Cruzeiro from 1993 to 1994, scoring 44 goals in 47 games.
  • In 1994, Ronaldo is selected to play the FIFA World Cup in the USA. Aged 17, Ronaldo helps Brazil win its 4th World Cup title.
  • After the 1994 World Cup, Ronaldo joins PSV Eindhoven (NED), where he scored 54 goals in 58 games.
  • In 1996, Ronaldo signs with FC Barcelons (SPA) and stays there until 1997, scoring 47 goals in 19 games. In 1996, Ronaldo is awarded the FIFA World Player of the Year award, aged 20.
  • In 1997, Ronaldo signs with Inter Milan (ITA), staying there until 2002. In 1997, Ronaldo is again awarded the FIFA World Player of the Year award. During his time at Inter Milan, Ronaldo had 2 serious knee injuries that sidelined him for a total of 2 seasons. However, Ronaldo returned to his best form for the 2002 World Cup, which he won and was Golden Boot. He played 99 games and scored 59 goals in Inter Milan colours.
  • In 2002, Ronaldo signs with Real Madrid (SPA), staying there until 2007, scoring over 100 goals.
  • In 2007, Ronaldo moves to AC Milan (ITA), playing there for 2 seasons, before returning to Brazil, playing for Corinthians until 2011, when Ronaldo retired.
